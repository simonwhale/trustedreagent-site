It's not just about the numbers

A high volume of reviews alone isn't enough. Our algorithm looks at the full picture — quality, consistency, engagement, and how an agent's reputation is trending over time.

Recent reviews carry more weight than older ones. An office that delivered five-star service three years ago but has slipped since won't score the same as one that's delivering consistently right now. We call this review decay — because trust is earned today, not banked from the past.

We also look at the balance between volume and quality. A handful of perfect reviews tells a different story to hundreds of reviews with a strong but realistic spread. Neither is automatically better — the algorithm considers both in context.

Reply rates matter too. When a client takes the time to leave feedback, how an agent responds says as much about their service as the review itself. Agents who engage with their reviews — positive and negative — score more favourably than those who don't.

Finally, we factor in multi-platform presence. An agent with strong reviews across Google, Trustpilot, Facebook and other platforms demonstrates broader, more credible trust than one that's only visible in a single place.

The exact weightings and thresholds are proprietary — they're reviewed and adjusted regularly to reflect how consumers actually use review data. What matters is that the methodology rewards agents who are genuinely, consistently delivering for their clients right now.
