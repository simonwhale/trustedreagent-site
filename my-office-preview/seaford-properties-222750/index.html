The methodology

Areas to keep sharpening.

These are the dimensions corporate marketing departments obsess over. They're learnable — and Kerfuffle membership puts them in your hands.

Review volume

More reviews, more signal.

Volume is one of the largest weights in the methodology. The single highest-leverage habit is asking every completed transaction for a review, every time, without exception. A steady drip of fresh reviews compounds faster than any one campaign.

The volume of feedback matters

Average star rating

Hold the standard, raise the average.

Average rating is a meaningful signal in the methodology too. The discipline is consistency across every transaction — every five-star adds to the average; every avoidable three-star drags it down. Service standards and how clients feel through the process is what shows up here.

Volume isn't everything

Reply rate

Every review answered earns points.

Public replies are scored as a separate signal — they show prospective clients how you handle praise and friction. Less-than-five-star reviews are where reputation is forged: a thoughtful, named response to a three-star turns a wobble into a credibility moment that the next 50 readers will see.
