The levers that move the needle

There's real room to climb — and the moves aren't expensive.

From the analysis: in the Wirral cohort, the gap between a 4.5★ and a 4.7★ rating is roughly four months of consistent operational discipline on three things. Not budget.

Reply to every review, every time

Wirral median reply rate is 54%. Top quartile is 80%+. Google's algorithm rewards reply rate independently of star rating. Most Wirral peers do this badly — closing the gap is a quick win.

Ask for the review at the right moment

106 reviews over your trading history is good but not stellar — peers at the top of the cohort are above 200. The trigger moment matters: end of tenancy walk-through, day of completion, the first month-in check-in.

Diversify the platforms

Your reputation lives almost entirely on Google. AllAgents, Feefo, Trustpilot — each one is a separate signal Google factors into local rankings. Diversifying isn't extra work, it's the same review request to a different inbox.

Surface your team's individual reputations

Your recent reviews already name Claire, Kurtis, Joe, and Laura Veste. That's gold. Personal-named reviews convert harder than agency-named ones — but they only matter if they're surfaced consistently on your own pages and on third-party platforms.
