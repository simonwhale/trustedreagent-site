### UK Estate Agent Reviews & Performance вҖ” 2026 Report

**Published by:** Trusted Agent (a Kerfuffle initiative)
**Date:** 17 May 2026
**Coverage:** 20,061 UK estate agent offices, of which 19,239 (95.1%) have validated Google review data. Performance data covers a two-year window (May 2024 вҖ“ May 2026), sourced from TwentyEA Insight. ## In plain English (read first)

- **Office** = one branch (e.g. "Belvoir Brighton")
- **Company** = the brand, all its offices together (e.g. "Belvoir")
- **Listings per office (2-year window)** = how many properties one office takes on for sale in a year. The industry calls this "new instructions".
- **Withdrawn** = a property the agent listed that came off the market without selling. High % = bad for the agent.
- **Reply rate** = how often the agent writes a reply to their Google reviews.
- **Trusted Agent Winner** = an agent in the top 10% of our rating model. Specific names are confidential until 7 July 2026.

## The headline findings

### 1. The "Boutique Agent Effect" вҖ” why a perfect 5вҳ… rating doesn't mean more business

When you group estate agents by their average Google star rating, the highest-rated band (4.9-5.0вҳ…) actually does **less** business than the band just below it (4.7-4.9вҳ…). The reason isn't quality вҖ” it's review volume.

**Grouped view (the cleanest headline):**

| Company's average Google rating | Listings per office (2-year window) |
|---|---|
| Under 4.0вҳ… | 64 |
| 4.0вҖ“4.5вҳ… | 142 |
| **4.5вҳ…+** | **196** |

The 4.5вҳ…+ band processes **77% more business per office than agents below 4.5вҳ…, and roughly 3Г— more than agents below 4вҳ….**

**Unfiltered view (showing the boutique band):**

| Company's average Google rating | Listings per office (2-year window) |
|---|---|
| Under 3.0вҳ… | 36 |
| 3.0вҖ“3.5вҳ… | 52 |
| 3.5вҖ“4.0вҳ… | 76 |
| 4.0вҖ“4.3вҳ… | 118 |
| 4.3вҖ“4.5вҳ… | 171 |
| 4.5вҖ“4.7вҳ… | 194 |
| **4.7вҖ“4.9вҳ…** | **222** вҶҗ peak |
| 4.9вҖ“5.0вҳ… | 160 вҶҗ boutique band

**The filtered picture вҖ” the "Boutique Agent Effect" revealed:**

Once you control for how many reviews each company has, the gap disappears:

| Company hasвҖҰ | Listings/office/yr if rated **4.9-5.0вҳ…** | Listings/office/yr if rated **4.7-4.9вҳ…** |
|---|---|---|
| Under 50 reviews | 75 | 113 |
| 50-100 reviews | 169 | 181 |
| 100-200 reviews | 232 | 241 |
| **200+ reviews** | **304** | **325** |

The 5вҳ… band looks weaker overall because **45% of its companies have under 50 reviews** вҖ” small, boutique operators where one or two perfect early reviews pin the score at 5.0. In the 4.7-4.9вҳ… band, only 21% are in that small-review category.

**Consumer takeaway:** A 5.0вҳ… rating on 30 reviews tells you very little. A 4.8вҳ… on 300 reviews tells you a lot. The single best predictor of an agent's transaction volume is the **combination** of high rating AND high review count.

**For boutique operators (typically below 50 reviews) this is actually a feature, not a bug** вҖ” they're deliberately operating at lower volumes, and their perfect ratings reflect genuine attention to each client. Just don't confuse them with the large-scale market leaders.

### 2. The retention gap

How an agent's rating relates to whether their listings actually sell. "Withdrawn" means the property came off the market without selling вҖ” the agent took it on, but never closed a deal on it. "Retained" is the share that did go through.

| Company's average Google rating | % retained (sold) | % withdrawn (unsold) |
|---|---|---|
| Under 3.0вҳ… | 33.7% | 66.3% |
| 3.0вҖ“3.5вҳ… | 37.0% | 63.0% |
| 3.5вҖ“4.0вҳ… | 38.1% | 61.9% |
| 4.0вҖ“4.3вҳ… | 42.5% | 57.5% |
| 4.3вҖ“4.5вҳ… | 51.4% | 48.6% |
| 4.5вҖ“4.7вҳ… | 54.8% | 45.2% |
| **4.7вҖ“4.9вҳ…** | **56.3%** | **43.7%** |
| 4.9вҖ“5.0вҳ… | 53.5% | 46.5% |

**The money quote:** Top-rated agents (4.5вҳ…+) **retain 29% more listings** than agents below 4.5вҳ… вҖ” their listings actually sell, instead of coming off the market unsold. At the extremes, the gap is even sharper: 4.7вҖ“4.9вҳ… agents retain **67% more listings** than agents rated below 3вҳ….

### 3. The reply rate signal вҖ” the mark of an engaged agent

| % of reviews replied to | Listings per office (2-year window) | % of listings withdrawn |
|---|---|---|
| 1вҖ“20% | 171 | 47.9% |
| 20вҖ“50% | 190 | 48.9% |
| 50вҖ“80% | 185 | 46.1% |
| 80вҖ“100% | 189 | 44.3% |

**What this means:** Reply rate is a softer signal than star rating, but far from neutral. Agents who reply to 80%+ of their reviews retain about **7% more listings** than agents who reply to under 20%. More importantly, replying correlates strongly with carrying a higher star rating in the first place вҖ” it's part of the same engaged-agent profile.

The real value of reply rate is what it signals to **future clients reading reviews** and to **Google's local-search ranking**. An office that consistently replies signals attentiveness; one that ignores reviews signals indifference. The data shows the kind of agent who engages with feedback also happens to be the kind who closes more deals.

## Method notes

- **Source data:** Google Places API validation across the full UK estate agent universe (95.1% match rate after a full revalidation pass). TwentyEA Insight Sales Competitive Landscape for transaction data.
- **Time period:** Two-year window, May 2024 вҖ“ May 2026. Chosen over a single-year cut to smooth annual market variability. All per-office listings figures are totals across the two-year window.
- **Coverage:** All UK postcode areas. England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland included.
- **Cleaning:** Non-trading and virtual-office registered addresses (e.g. 128 City Road, N1 6DX) excluded, alongside a small set of non-estate-agent property businesses.
- **Joining method:** Brand Г— postcode area. One company operating in two postcode areas is counted twice; this is intentional, because the TwentyEA data reports market share separately for each postcode area.
- **Sample sizes available on request** for any specific finding.
